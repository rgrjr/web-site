Thought on choice of UI toolkit

Home : ICanCAD : UI Tools

It seems to me that there are strong parallels between ICanCAD parts and defs on the one hand, and CLIM output records on the other. We could exploit this by designing the appropriate low-level ICanCAD support code so that it can be used later to support a CLIM clone as well.

Similarities Differences
Both are hierarchical, in that output records are contained within other output records. (See Note 1 below.) Hierarchy in ICanCAD is shared, with named defs potentially appearing in more than one place in the hierarchy. (This suggests a possible CLIM extension, but it's probably not worth the effort of supporting multiple parents for an output record.)
Both use similar 2D geometrical concepts such as relative placement through transforms, and bounding boxes. The stream orientation of CLIM introduces certain biases, such as top-to-bottom construction and the strong presumption of non-overlap. A solution that is general enough for ICanCAD may not suit CLIM well.
Sophisticated incremental display update boosts the performance of both ICanCAD (during editing tasks) and CLIM (during incremental update of AVV dialogs). Again, the stream orientation of CLIM may interfere.

See also the Free CLIM page.

Notes

Note 1: In fact, one could argue that ICanCAD hierarchy should be more like the CLIM hierarchy, in at least one respect. Schematic uses could stand to be output-record style collections of one-off component records for labels and pins (and anything else in the def that is context dependent). Pity this can't be per-window . . .
