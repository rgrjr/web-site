Crow Hill, Princeton and Westminster 6/27/02

Landowner: Massachusetts Dept. of Environmental Management 978-874-2303

R.Doucette, E.Engberg, D.Goguen, D.Melanson, A.Rubin, A.Robinson, Y.Fujita, J.Grote

Crow Hill is located in Leominster State Forest (www.state.ma.us/dem/parks/lmsf.htm), the northwestern-most area covered in this book. It has arguably the best climbing in Massachusetts; from easy topropes to harder lead climbs, a couple that can go two pitches in length. On rare occasion, an ice route may form. Crow Hill “was the scene for ambitious members of the AMC in the late twenties and thirties…In the mid-sixties, while the big aid routes of Yosemite lurked in the back of everyone's mind, Crow Hill became a practice ground…(some of the harder crack routes of that day) fell to the hands of eastern climbers who distinguished themselves in The Valley.” (Climbing in Eastern Massachusetts by Hendrick & Streibert, 1975). First ascentionists of the late 60s and early 70s include Henry Barber, Sam Streibert, Bob Anderson, Steve Arsenault, Steve Hendrick and Ed Webster. There has been a recent (1999-2000) flourish of activity on a few extreme trad leads by Peter Vintoniv/Tim Kemple and Barry Rugo/Mark Richey. It is one of the few crags where first ascent information has been recorded. Crow Hill's southeast facing gneiss cliff is 650' long by 80' high. It is known for hard routes with easy grades. People fall on 5.7s and break bones here. Some climbs will be dirty after a rain. The cracks may weep in the spring. It can be hot in the summer and buggy in the spring. Fixed gear is from the first accents or replacement of first ascent gear. If there are toprope anchors at the top of a climb please use them, as the trees are taking a beating. You may need long (10-20') slings where there are no anchors.

Getting there: Take route 2 west to route 31 south (exit 28). Go south 1.3 miles, past Park HQ (978-874-2303) on your right, where you should stop and get your free climbing permit. Go another 1.3 miles to a gravel climbers' parking lot on your right (past the paved parking lot on your left). Take the trail from the back of the parking lot, where a less than 10 minute walk brings you to the left end of the cliff. The Midstate Trail goes up the left side (and along the top) of the cliff. Just right of the trail is an obvious open book. This is Layback at the left end of the “End Crags”.

Crack in the Woods (no topo, 50' in the woods to the left of the descent trail) [Locate this]

  1. Unnamed 5.10+ Toprope. Thin crack on the left side goes over a slight bulge.

  2. Unnamed 5.9 Climb a thin flake and a bulge. (FA Eric Engberg and Alain Comeau 1970s)

End Crags

  1. Unnamed 5.5

  2. Layback 5.6 Obvious open book.

  3. Tom's Dilemma 5.5+

  4. Melissa's Madness 5.10 Toprope.

  5. Beginner's Blessing 5.4 Three inch crack in an obvious corner.

  6. Pine Tree 5.4-5.5 Fifteen ft. or so to the right.

The next two routes are half way between the End Crags and the Green Corner area.

  1. Recess Left 5.7 The face on the left side of the big wet cave.

  2. Monkey Business 5.10. At the back of the cave. Climb the inside corner, roof and dihedral. (Formerly The Recess A1.)

Green Corner

  1. The Wall 5.7-5.10 Numerous boulder problems. Great hand traverse.

  2. Bet you can't 5.8 The crack in the middle of The Wall.

The next 4 routes begin below and just right a large stump, to the right of the boulder traverse.

  1. Tiny Pitches 5.7 Climb up to the tree stump and go left.

  2. Sun Gypsy 5.6 Up to the tree stump, go a little left, then up through the triple overhangs, exit right. (FA Steve Devlin and Craig Somerville April 1972)

  3. Green Gremlin 5.6 (using the tree) Right of the stump, up the corner past 2 birches and a hemlock tree.

  4. Fireplace 5.9 Same start, but go left [or right? If left, move to #5] of corner straight up the arrete. (FA Dan Melanson and Dave Goguen TR)

  5. Green Route 5.5* Go right and up after the stump (crossing over “The Block”, see below) and traverse 60' along huge ledge, then up a beautiful open book. Usually done in two pitches. A couple of variations to the finish.

  6. Five Easy Pieces 5.10 Climb the short face up to the platform common to the “Green” routes. Pitch 2 Climb the face to the right of the Green Gremlin to the top. (FA Tom Bowker/Dave Goguen Summer 1996)

Fisherman's Wall

  1. The Block 5.11 Climb up the block that makes up the far left end of Fisherman's Wall. Short and hard.

  2. Unnamed 5.11+ Climb the off width crack between The Block and Fisherman's Wall. Then climb the outside corner. Ends at the Green Route traverse ledge.

  3. Unnamed. There is a very long and pumpy 40' mostly low traverse on the left side of Fisherman's Wall. It starts from the left of Topaz and ends at Cromagnon. The crux is a high crossing at the start of Diagonal.

  4. Topaz 5.9* Just left of Babe. Two pitches. Second pitch is hard. (FA Henry Barber 1974)

  5. Babe 5.9 15' left of Diagonal. Dirty, never climbed. (FA Henry Barber and Bob Anderson April 1972)

  6. Strawberry Fields 5.9 Crosses Diagonal, on which most people finish.

  7. Diagonal 5.8+* The obvious left-leaning hand crack. (FA Kevin Bein and Bob Hall 1967)

  8. Wild Women in Love 5.10 On the ledge at the top of Babe/Diagonal, follow the thin crack.

  9. Absolute 5.13R/X Start up the seam right of Diagonal. Two small nuts are needed. After you leave the crack, grab a sidepull and dyno for a horizontal. Continue up and left to another horizontal crack (crux). More 5.12 climbing leads to the top. (FFA Peter Vintoniv and Tim Kemple, 2000)

  10. Cromagnon 5.10* A steep start (3 options, right easiest) leads to hand jams above. A difficult traverse left (you will be pumped) leads to the Green Route dihedral. (Formerly aid route called Overhang. FA Steve Arsenault 1967. FFA Sam Streibert and Dennis Merrit 1971)

  11. It Doesn't Matter 5.13a (formerly Cromagnon Linkup 5.10 A2). Free climb Cromagnon crack to the start of the left trending traverse. Pass the rivet ladder, placing wired nuts on the rivets on lead, go up and right to 2 horizontal cracks that are used to gain the top. (FFA Tim Kemple/[w/Vintoniv?] 2000) [confirm]

  12. Cromagnon Woman 5.10 After second Cromagnon bolt, diagonal up left via undercling and lieback.

  13. Fishermans Folly A5 Was downgraded to A3 after it was bolted by those “who felt it important to only reach the top”. Bolts gone, its A5 again, never climbed. (FA Steve Hendrick and Henry Barber September 1971)

  14. Dune 5.12-5.13R/X Wicked hard, with tricky gear placements. Start just left of Jane, passing over the roof. Traverse left 10' then up the face trending right (crux) to a good hold. Continue up 10' then left toward 2 pins in a horizontal. Then up right to another pin, and finaly up left on small edges to the top. Follows almost the same line as Fishermans Folly. (FFA maybe John Mallery long ago, after bolting. Bolts then chopped. Mark Richey/Barry Rugo climbed it on preplaced gear 1999, then Peter Vintinov/Tim Kemple lead it from the ground up 1999)

  15. Jane 5.11* Painfully obvious finger crack through a bulge to piton anchor. Originally climbed to the top but most lower from anchor above crux. When first freed, it may have been one of the hardest pitches in the US. (FA Steve Arsenault spring 1966. FFA Henry Barber spring 1972)

  16. Cheetah 5.10-* Start at the Tarzan corner, then left above Jane, following easier 5.8 cracks past a birch tree and peg to the top. [Does this route merge with Jane or what?]

  17. Old Crow 5.11R/X Starts in corner, climbs left of Tarzan (pin), then crosses over it, right, up a steep dihedral past more old pins.

Buttress

  1. Tarzan 5.8 Find the easiest way up the pair of cracks in the corner. Above traverse left via the left crack, then a ramp with poison ivy. Finish by passing right under the big roof. (FA Bob Hall/Kevin Bein)

  2. Fool on a Hill 5.9* Face climb right of the Tarzan corner, past 2 bolts to a chain anchor. One set of toprope/rappel chains serves Fool on a Hill and Buttress. (FA Henry Barber & Bob Anderson April 1972)

  3. Lizard's Head 5.11 This steep crack is found on the ledge up and left of Fool on a Hill, which it can be linked with. (FA Ed Webster December 1973 as A2)

  4. Buttress 5.9 Start in a shallow right facing corner 20' right of Fool. Zig zag between rounded edges and slopers to chain anchor. Continues up past a bolt, but most parties lower from chain. (FA Hugo Stadmuller and John Porter 1963)

  5. Bawana 5.11 Toprope. Starts at the arete right of Buttress. Then joins it at the rap ledge.

  6. Watusi 5.9* Begin at a short 4' crack and start left (past a pin) or straight up to a 3'x2' block below a horizontal crack. Continue up past 2 more horizontals to finish on a grassy ledge, modern gear needed. [alternate description: start in broken corner in center of buttress, climb to a niche, exit right around block, climb thin slab above then follow leaning crack right and up to terrace.] (FA Sam Streibert and Steve Arsenault December 1968)

  7. Birds of Fire 5.9 (FA Ed Webster January 1974)

  8. The Nose 5.9* Start up the upper dihedral of chimney, but move left around the edge at horizontal and up a short crack. Main Face, Left

  1. The Chimney 5.6 Climb a right leaning crack to a left leaning ramp. Belay in the bushes. Pitch 2 follows the ramp up left, then finish in a dihedral (sorta like a “chimney”).

  2. Outertsite 5.7 Just left of the Blue Route is a more difficult crack. Briefly join Blue Route. Second pitch goes up and right in a steep wall.

  3. Blue Route 5.4 The most obvious (and easy) route up and left from the start of Intertwine. Often a hornet nest can be found above the bushes. Two pitches. Finish via Chimney.

  4. Intertwine 5.8* Obvious right leaning crack in the face. Fingerlocks and good pro lead to a pumpy exit move. Rappel from a threaded chain at the back wall (poison ivy), or exit right via Post Road. (FA Sam Streibert and Larry Winship autumn 1967. FFA Bob Anderson April 1972)

  5. Summer Wishes 5.9 Climb the face right of Intertwine, past two pins, joining Intertwine near the top. The direct, independent finish is 5.11.

The next three routes leave the ledge above Intertwine.

  1. Blue Angel 5.8 “Weird steep face climb” right of finish to Outersite, left of the overhang. (FA Bob Anderson spring 1972)

  2. Panic 5.9+R/X Awkward overhanging inside corner up and left of Intertwine chain. (FA Bob Anderson and Sam Streibert August 1972)

  3. White Courage 5.10-5.11 Toprope. The bulge above Intertwine.

  4. Blow Up 5.9 Up and right of Interwine chain, follow a ramp up to a break in the roof above. (FA Sam Streibert and Bob Anderson September 1972)

Main Face, Right

  1. Karen's Folly 5.8 Up the wide groove which delineates the left side of this face. Pass the huge block on the left. The exit move is hard.

  2. Zip Code 5.11 Toprope. Up the groove and through the roof formed by the huge chockstone. A good start is the 5.9 rounded arrete, right of the groove.

  3. Post Road 5.7 Up the groove, “exit from under the ominous ceiling to the right. Jam or layback to the flaring crack above.” Finish up the inside corner/face above and right or traverse left to the Intertwine chains and rappel. (FA John Post and others 1952)

  4. Franny's Folly 5.7* Start just right of Post Road, climb up and right into left facing corner. (Direct start is 5.10ish) Traverse right into another left facing corner which is followed to the large ledge. Finish as for Post Road. Good practice at managing rope drag. (FA Dave Helman, Paul Doyle and Jim Gustafson 1963) A 5.9 variation gains the second corner from directly below.

  5. Thin Line 5.8* Follow a strenuous steep crack (2 pins) to horizontal cracks and the top. (FA Robert Park and Steve Hendrick May 1972. FFA Ed Webster September 1973)

  6. Smoky 5.9 A variation of Thin Line that starts at mid height, goes right, around flake and up.

  7. Bev's Bewilderment 5.11c Bucket to tricky face moves. (FA Henry Barber and Tom Hayden 1971 as A5)

  8. After the Goldrush 5.10 This balancy start is a variation of Bev's Bewilderment. Friction traverse to the right then mantle to a small ledge shared with Recitavist. Then left via hand traverse.

  9. The Recitavist 5.11+* Climb the thin diagonal crack on the right sighe of the face to a ledge. Climb the thin vertical crack and finish up the face. Climbers are not known for their spelling. (FFA Henry Barber November 1972)

  10. Second's Nightmare 5.6 A2 Starts at the vertical crack at the right end of the face. Aid above the crack, right around the corner. Almost never done. (FA Bob Hall and W.Hastings June 1966)
