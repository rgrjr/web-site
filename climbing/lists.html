Local and regional mailing lists:

climbing@rgrjr.dyndns.org
I started managing this list in 2001 for discussion and announcements for Boston area climbers; it is still quite low volume. For one thing, I use it to post late-breaking changes to the Tuesday Night North Shore climbing schedule. To subscribe, send mail to climbing-subscribe@rgrjr.dyndns.org; neither the subject nor the content is important.

To post a message, send mail to climbing@rgrjr.dyndns.org. To reduce spam, there are three requirements for messages posted to this list:

  1. The message must be directly addressed to the list, i.e. no blind carbon copies (BCC's).
  2. The message must be formatted as "plain text" (as opposed to HTML). Add climbing@rgrjr.dyndns.org to your address book, then select "Plain text" as the preferred mail format value. (In Mozilla, this is the item labelled "Prefers to recieve mail messages formatted as:" when you edit an address book entry.)
  3. The message must not contain attachments.

You do not need to be a subscriber in order to post.

[This list has been experiencing technical difficulties lately; please stay tuned. -- rgr, 5-Sep-04.]

The White Mountain Report
Every Thursday, Al Hospers broadcasts this summary of White Mountain climbing conditions, news, and weather, frequently including goodies such as a discount coupon from International Mountain Equipment in North Conway. From the report itself:
Please tell others about The White Mountain Report. Feel free to email a copy to your friends and encourage them to subscribe, it's easy. Click here: 
       http://www.neclimbs.com/wmr_subscribe.html
Enter your email address and away you'll go. You will get an immediate confirmation and the very next White Mountain Report. You can also Unsubscribe using the same link.
The web site always features the current edition of the newsletter, in case you would like to check it out.
