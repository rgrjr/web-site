General

When and where is the course?

It will be held Saturday and Sunday the 18th and 19th of September, at Quincy Quarries in the Blue Hills Reservation, Quincy, Massachusetts. Instruction will begin promptly at 8:30 AM, rain or shine, transitioning into toproping in the afternoon as time and weather permit.

What are the benefits of taking this course?

In addition to meeting fellow outdoor climbing enthusiasts, you will learn to set up toprope anchors using natural and other clifftop anchor points, and to clean bolted anchors. Furthermore, students who graduate from this program will be invited to join the AMC's Fall Frolic climbing weekend in the Gunks September 25-26. While most of the climbing there is multipitch "trad" (i.e. requiring gear placement), the Gunks has toproping opportunities in a beautiful setting with world-class climbing. This is one of the reasons why you should want to learn to climb outdoors. See the Mohonk Preserve (which owns the land) and the Gunks Climbers Coalition for more climbing info.

Where is the survey I need to complete?

It's at https://forms.gle/WWfX3dBPnPruC8UV8. Registration and survey must be completed by Sunday, August 22.

Is there a course fee?

Yes, $10. Scholarships are available. Payment will be required after your application is accepted. You will be notified some time after the application closing date on Sunday, August 22. Those who are not accepted will be wait-listed in case of cancellations prior to September 10.

What should I bring?

In addition to being appropriately dressed for the weather, you will need to bring the following personal climbing equipment:

  • Climbing helmet
  • Climbing harness
  • Belay device (ATC-style)
  • Three locking carabiners
  • Double-length nylon sling

If providing any of this presents a financial hardship, please contact the leaders to make alternate arrangements.
