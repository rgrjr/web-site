Boston Climbing: Frequently asked questions

Q: How do I join the Tuesday Night group?

A: Just show up and introduce yourself; there's no formal notion of "joining."

Q: I don't have my own top rope equipment ...

A: That's OK, as long has you have your personal gear (shoes, harness, belay device). Since most people have ropes, but only half can be climbing at any one time, we usually have more than enough ropes.

Q: When and where do you guys usually meet?

A: The directions on the "Directions to top-rope areas" page for the Tuesday crags will take you right to the crag we climb on. Usually, we are pretty obvious -- the largest group, if not the only climbers present. Since most people come directly from work, the earliest arrivals are at 5:00 or 6:00, depending on location and traffic.

Q: What do you think is the best option to meet other climbers in the Boston Area?

A: If you can get out of Boston, the Boston Rock Gym is a great place to meet people. There's a commuter rail station about a half mile away, and an express bus from Haymarket to Cummings Park, which is about a half mile on the other side, but no other public transit to speak of. Gravity Rock Gym is easier to get to by the T, but I haven't yet visited myself.

In good weather, there's usually a crowd of people bouldering at the Alcove of Hammond Pond, [finish. -- rgr, 18-Sep-04.]

The C Wall at the Quincy Quarries is a magnet on sunny winter days, as it is sheltered from the wind on three sides and gets extra sunlight reflected off the pond below the slab, which makes it the warmest outdoor climbing location around, sometime 20 degrees above the surrounding air temperature.

The The MIT Outing Club (MITOC) is another good resource, especially for students. They have irregularly-scheduled rock gym trips, and I hear they will even let Harvard people join.
