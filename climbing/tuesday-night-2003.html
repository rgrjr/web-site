Tuesday Night North Shore climbing schedule

Home : Climbing : Tuesday Night schedule

This is a semi-official activity of the Boston Chapter Mountaineering Committee (BCMC) of the Boston Chapter of the Appalachian Mountain Club (AMC). Those of us who work and live on the North Shore find it hard to get to the Quincy Quarries for the traditional after-work Wednesday climbing events, so we have our own tradition of Tuesday night climbing. Since there are no large climbing areas within an easy drive, we rotate the location among a series of smaller sites according to the following schedule. Everyone is welcome, including beginners, but we cannot provide safety instruction, so if you are not confident of your belaying skills, please bring your own instructor.

Please bring your top-rope gear (ropes, long slings, and anchor stuff) in addition to your personal climbing equipment. (Small wires or camming devices may be helpful at some of the crags, if you have them.) We often retire afterward to a nearby watering hole for beer and sustenance.

When it rains on Tuesday we sometimes try to regroup at the same location on Wednesday. Those with less flexible schedules sometimes go to the Boston Rock Gym on rainy Tuesdays. Alternate plans are often discussed on the climbing at rgrjr.dyndns.org mailing list, including whether the rain has stopped early enough to make it worthwhile. In any case, there is always climbing at the Quincy Quarries on Wednesdays (if it dries up in time).

Footnote: Lynn Woods access

As of a few years ago, climbing on Lynn Tower requires advance permission from the appropriate town committee. We were unable to renew our permission in time for the scheduled 29 April date, and have therefore substituted Black and White Rocks. Although this was the previous Tuesday's destination, it was rained out. Hopefully, we will fare better on the 29th.

Footnote: Lynn Woods access, continued

Once again, I didn't get my act together in time to get permission to climb on 23 September. I apologize, and promise I'll do better next year -- Lynn Tower is one of my favorite places, and I'd hate to have to drop it from the schedule.
