Tuesday Night North Shore climbing schedule

Home : Climbing : Tuesday Night schedule

This is a semi-official activity of the Boston Chapter Mountaineering Committee (BCMC) of the Boston Chapter of the Appalachian Mountain Club (AMC). Those of us who work and live on the North Shore find it hard to get to the Quincy Quarries for the traditional after-work Wednesday climbing events, so we have our own tradition of Tuesday night climbing. Since there are no large climbing areas within an easy drive, we rotate the location among a series of smaller sites according to the following schedule. Everyone is welcome, including beginners, but we cannot provide safety instruction, so if you are not confident of your belaying skills, please bring your own instructor.

Please bring your top-rope gear (ropes, long slings, and anchor stuff; small wires or camming devices may be helpful at some of the crags). We often retire afterward to a nearby watering hole for beer and sustenance.

When it rains on Tuesday we sometimes try to regroup at the same location on Wednesday. Those with less flexible schedules sometimes meet at the Boston Rock Gym the same night. Alternate plans are often discussed on climbing at rgrjr.dyndns.org, including whether the rain has stopped early enough to make it worthwhile. In any case, there is always climbing at the Quincy Quarries on Wednesdays (if it dries up in time).

Footnote: Lynn Woods access

As of a few years ago, climbing on Lynn Tower requires advance permission from the appropriate town committee. (The restriction may or may not apply to Lynn Woods generally; we hope to clarify this shortly.) We had obtained permission to climb Lynn Tower when this rule went into force, but due to a miscommunication, we were unaware that we needed to renew our permission each year, and did not find this out in time for the 2 July date. Consequently, we have had to move the 2 July climbing venue to Hammond Pond, pond area.

We apologize for the inconvenience, and hope we can secure permission for Lynn Tower in time for 17 September, the next scheduled Lynn Tower date. Please be sure to check this page for updates before showing up at the Tower on that day.

Update: Lynn Woods access

In the event, we failed to secure permission to climb Lynn Tower. The consensus of the Tuesday Nighters who showed up at Black and White Rocks on 10 September was to replace Lynn Tower with an additional night at Black and White Rocks. The thought was that nobody who was unable to make the 10 September date would be likely to complain that there were too many B&W nights, and none of those present objected. And the sooner the decision was made and publicised, the better.
